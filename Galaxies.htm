Galaxies

Galaxies consist of stars, planets, and vast clouds of gas and dust, all bound together by gravity.

Milky Way

The Milky Way is the galaxy that includes the Solar System, with the name describing the galaxy's appearance from Earth: a hazy band of light seen in the night sky formed from stars that cannot be individually distinguished by the naked eye.

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy is a barred spiral galaxy and is the nearest major galaxy to the Milky Way. It was originally named the Andromeda Nebula and is cataloged as Messier 31, M31, and NGC 224. Andromeda has a diameter of about 46.56 kiloparsecs and is approximately 765 kpc from Earth.

Whirlpool

This is a spiral galaxy in the constellation Canes Venatici with a Seyfert 2 active galactic nucleus. It's the first galaxy classified as a spiral galaxy, located 32 million light-years away and measuring 109,000 light-years in diameter.

Black Eye Galaxy

The Black Eye Galaxy is a spiral galaxy located 17 million light-years away in the constellation Coma Berenices. Discovered independently by Edward Pigott and Johann Elert Bode in 1779, as well as by Charles Messier the following year.

Cartwheel Galaxy

The Cartwheel Galaxy is a lenticular ring galaxy about 500 million light-years away in the constellation Sculptor. It has a D₂₅ isophotal diameter of 44.23 kiloparsecs, and a mass of about 2.9–4.8 × 10⁹ solar masses; its outer ring has a circular velocity of 217 km/s. It was discovered by Fritz Zwicky in 1941.

Cigar Galaxy

Cigar Galaxy, also known as the Messier 82 galaxy, is a starburst galaxy situated about 12 million light-years away in the Ursa Major constellation. It's the second-largest member of the M81 Group, with an isophotal diameter of 12.52 kiloparsecs.
